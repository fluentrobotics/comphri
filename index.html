ROB 498 /599: Computational Human-Robot Interaction

Instructor: Christoforos Mavrogiannis (cmavro at umich dot edu)

Lectures: TuThu 15:00 - 16:30 (1060 FMCRB)

Office hours: TBD

Course description: This is an advanced course, covering computational techniques that enable robots to work with and around people. Topics will include prediction, planning, and control approaches, discussed in the context of applications like crowd navigation and collaborative manipulation. Besides algorithmic foundations, the course will explore topics in experiment design, discussing evaluation methodologies that will enable smooth deployment of robots in human environments.

Prerequisites: There are no formal prerequisites but mathematical maturity (e.g., ROB 101, Math 215, IOE 265) and programming background (e.g., ROB 320 or EECS 281) are expected. A foundation on the design of human-robot systems (e.g., ROB 204) are recommended.
